Campus is an online, accredited community college inspired by those seeking to increase completion rates for community college students. In efforts to address how systemic barriers and unique personal responsibilities may hinder many students completing their degree, Campus' vision is a space where students' multitude of needs are met so that they may find their own success within any higher education program they take on.

Campuswire is the complete home base for teaching and learning in college classes. Used by over 500k students and professors across the nation, Campuswire combines a modern Q&A forum, chatrooms, and direct messages to simplify communication; live video and in-class polling to make online teaching an engaging experience for students; and the capabilities of a LMS and SIS to manage student records.

web app

I developed a visual analysis of our brand evolution over the years, which we referenced in order to develop Invention Corps' newest brand version. As the classic colors of the organization, red, yellow, green, and blue were retained as the main colors while the art style evolved further.

We iterated through several versions of the site. The inital versions on the right were more centered on a one-page scroll with simplistic additional pages. After the first version of the website was published, we realized that we needed space for more content in order to provide enough information for both of our main stakeholder groups (prospective members and possible project partners). We therefore evolved to the version on the right, which features more art by club members, as well as parallax scrolling and snap to screen effects to optimize the user experience.

The format of the project page was one of our key challenges. We wanted to highlight our use of the design thinking process through all of our projects while still remaining impact-focused. From the version on the left, which included equal focus on all stages of the process, we pivoted to the version on the right, which highlights key points of the process while specifically spotlighting the deliverables. This created a focus on our tangible impact, which we felt was important to give stakeholders a better sense of what each project accomplished.

On our 'Work' project portfolio page, we created icons that would be replicable by future club members for sustainability reasons. We highlighted the deliverables, topics, and tools used for each project card in order to give stakeholders an at-a-glance impression of our collection of work.


For this three-month project, I was part of all three sub-teams: Design, Development, and Content. I took point on the UI/UX design of the site, focusing on layout and steps to optimize the user experience. From the development side, I worked the landing page and implemented various scrolling features in JavaScript. As a founding member, I led members to reflect on club values while encouraging evolution toward a new style.

Working in a team of nine, we were able to push out three versions of this website, evolving each version to accomodate a specific club need each time.
